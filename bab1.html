The Integrated Learning Project (ILP) for ninth graders is a comprehensive program designed to combine multiple subjects into a real-world application. This year, we were tasked with organizing a bazaar and a performing arts show, which required extensive planning, collaboration, and creativity. The project was officially started in July 2024, with each class being divided into small groups of 6-7 members, who were expected to work together until the final evaluation in April 2025. Our team, named AstanaKarya, was formed with the vision of showcasing Indonesia’s cultural heritage through our booth and products. The name "Astana" was chosen as it means "palace" in Sanskrit, symbolizing grandeur, while "Karya" was used to represent the creative works we aimed to present. Our logo, theme, and products were carefully selected to align with the overarching goal of promoting Indonesia’s diverse culture.

Throughout our planning, various product ideas were explored before the final selection was made. Initially, biotechnology-based milk buns using instant yeast were considered, but due to budget constraints, an alternative was chosen—recycled paper, which aligns with sustainability principles. Additionally, handcrafted batik hair bows were created, and custom-made stickers, pouches featuring Indonesian landmarks, and keychains inspired by local flora were produced. Food and beverage items such as waffles with different toppings and "Dieng Frizz," a mojito-inspired drink, were also introduced. To attract visitors, a booth based on Indonesia’s traditional market carts was meticulously designed. While initial plans included a stall-style setup, material limitations resulted in a simpler design, yet cultural elements such as traditional puppets and wooden wheels were maintained. An Instagram page was set up to showcase our products, and pre-orders were made available for customers.

Aside from the bazaar, participation in a performing arts show was required. Our team collaborated with another group, Yogi’s Treasure, to produce a musical drama that combined acting with live music. The performance was carefully planned to convey messages of friendship and cultural appreciation while incorporating elements of Indonesia’s traditions. Despite initial script and choreography challenges, a well-received final performance was delivered. The ILP was not just an academic exercise but a valuable learning experience where essential life skills were acquired. Insights into entrepreneurship, event management, teamwork, and adaptability were gained. Communication was recognized as a crucial factor in ensuring smooth collaboration, while problem-solving skills were developed to overcome challenges. Through hard work and perseverance, the complexities of the project were successfully navigated, and goals were ultimately achieved.
